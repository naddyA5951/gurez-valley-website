Why visit

Where Kashmir feels remote, slow, and wide open.

Gurez sits north of the main Kashmir Valley, close to the great Himalayan range. The Kishenganga, also known downstream as the Neelum River, runs through the valley while mountain walls and alpine fields shape every view.

The destination is known for Habba Khatoon peak, Dawar village, Tulail side trips, trout streams, Dard-Shin culture, and road journeys over Razdan Pass.

Top experiences

Build the trip around these places.

Use these stops as the backbone for a compact weekend escape or a slower valley journey.

Habba Khatoon Peak

The iconic pyramid-shaped mountain that frames many of Gurez Valley's most memorable views.

Kishenganga River

A glacier-fed river corridor for riverside walks, photography, bridge views, and trout country atmosphere.

Tulail Valley

A quieter sub-valley with wooden homes, open slopes, and village-to-village mountain scenery.

Suggested routes

Choose your pace.

These sample plans are designed for travelers starting from Srinagar or Bandipora.

2-Day Scenic Escape

1
Srinagar to Dawar via Bandipora and Razdan Pass

Start early, pause at pass viewpoints, reach Dawar by evening, and walk near the river before sunset.

2
Habba Khatoon viewpoint and return

Photograph the peak at morning light, visit nearby villages, then drive back with buffer time.

3-Day Valley Classic

1
Arrival in Dawar

Make the road journey the experience: Wular side, Bandipora, Razdan Pass, and first views of Gurez.

2
Habba Khatoon and river villages

Spend the full day around Dawar, the river, and local viewpoints without rushing the altitude.

3
Tulail side drive and departure

Explore deeper village landscapes if roads permit, then return toward Srinagar or stay another night.

5-Day Slow Travel

1
Transfer and acclimatize

Reach Dawar, keep the evening easy, and settle into the mountain rhythm.

2
Habba Khatoon and Dawar

Visit viewpoints, talk to local hosts, and keep time for photography near the river.

3
Tulail Valley day

Travel through wooden-house villages and mountain fields, with flexible stops along the route.

4
Local culture and leisure

Use the day for homestay food, slow walks, trout-side views, and weather buffer.

5
Return through Razdan Pass

Leave after breakfast and keep daylight for the mountain road back to Srinagar.

Travel essentials

Know before you go.

Season

Plan for late spring to autumn. Heavy snow can close Razdan Pass during winter months.

Route

The common road approach is Srinagar to Bandipora, then onward to Gurez via Razdan Pass.

Stay

Dawar is the most practical base for guesthouses, local stays, meals, and onward valley drives.

Carry

Pack warm layers, cash, offline maps, ID, medicines, and extra time for mountain-road delays.
